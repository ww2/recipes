Flourless Brownies (GF)

Based On: Flourless Fudgy Brownies by Brian Levy

Ingredients

  • 115g 68%..72% chocolate chips
  • 40g olive oil
  • 200g white sugar
  • 2 large eggs, room temperature
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 0.5 tsp fine salt
  • 100g almond flour
  • 60g natural cocoa powder
  • 0.5 tsp baking soda
  • 55g chopped pecans

Directions

Heat the oven to 350 F.

Line (mini-)muffin tin with parchment cups.

Melt the chocolate in a large heatproof measuring cup in the microwave on high in 10 to 15-second increments, stirring periodically.

Pour the melted chocolate into a large bowl, then stir in the sugar and olive oil until evenly combined. (It will still be grainy at this point.)

Whisk the eggs, vanilla, salt into the chocolate until evenly combined, and the result has a ribbonlike texture.

In a medium bowl, whisk the almond flour, cocoa powder, and baking soda. (You may need to sift the almond flour, to remove clumps.)

Pour the flour mixture into the chocolate mixture, and stir thoroughly until well combined.

Fold in chopped nuts until they are evenly distributed.

Using a small cookie scoop (mini muffins) or a spoon-like spatula (muffins), fill each parchment cup about ¾ full. A scant tablespoon (overfilled small cookie scoop) per mini-muffin cup is about right. Don't overfill cups; they will puff while baking (and may collapse during cooling if jostled).

Bake mini-muffins for 11-13 minutes, muffins 20-24 minutes until the tops look set and just barely lose their wet sheen. The centers should still look very slightly underdone — they'll firm up as they cool. Do not bake until fully set or they'll dry out.

Let the brownies cool in the tin for 30 minutes, then transfer the cups to a wire rack and cool completely, about 30 minutes.
