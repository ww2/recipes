Orange Cardamom Yogurt Cake

Based on: Orange Cardamom Yogurt Cake recipe at fiveandspice.wordpress.com

Ingredients

Cake

  • Miniature (6 cup) bundt pan, or standard (8.5" x 4.5") loaf pan
  • 1.5 cups all-purpose flour, plus more for dusting the pan
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp ground cardamom
  • 0.75 tsp kosher salt
  • zest grated from 1 medium orange (1 to 1.5 tsp)
  • 0.5 cup sugar
  • 0.75 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 0.5 cup olive oil
  • 2 large eggs
  • 0.5 tsp vanilla extract

Glaze

  • 3 tbsp yogurt, divided
  • 1.25 tsp ground cardamom
  • 90 grams powdered sugar

Directions

Cake

Preheat oven to 350 degrees F.

Thoroughly butter then pan, then dust with flour and tap out the excess.

In a small bowl, whisk together 1.5 cups flour, baking powder, cardamom, and kosher salt.

In a medium bowl, whisk together orange zest and sugar. Add in the yogurt, olive oil, eggs, and vanilla extract, and stir until well blended.

Fold in the dry ingredients until theyâ€™re combined.

Pour the batter into prepared pan and smooth the top. Tamp the pan on the counter several times, to reduce bubbles.

Bake in the center of the oven until a tester poked into center comes out clean -- 30-40 minutes for bundt pan, 50-55 minutes for loaf pan.

Let the cake cool in pan on a wire rack for 15 minutes. Then, run a knife around the edges to loosen it, invert it onto a cooling rack and allow to cool completely before glazing.

Glaze

Stir the cardamom into 2 tbsp yogurt until well combined.

Add in the powdered sugar and stir until completely combined -- the result should be smooth, but very stiff.

Add in a little more yogurt (or liquid whey, if that's available, a few drops at a time) and stir thoroughly; repeat until you reach the desired consistency.

Immediately apply glaze to cooled cake; pouring it out in one long slow stream yields the best looking glaze.

Notes

The glaze needs to be viscous, so that when poured from a spoon into the mixing bowl, it moves like old honey.

Original recipe used an 8.5" x 4.5" loaf pan, and baked for 50 to 55 minutes; I used a 6 cup bundt pan, which baked in 30 to 40 minutes.

Original recipe had 1 cup sugar, and no glaze.
